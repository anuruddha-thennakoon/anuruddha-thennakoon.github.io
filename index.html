Background

Fraud is a wrongful or criminal deception aimed to bring financial or personal gain. Due to the rapid growth in e-business and electronic payment systems, Fraud is rising in banking transactions associated with credit cards. With time number of fraud cases have been constantly increased and new forms of fraud have been introduced. Four main credit card frauds that have been addressed in this study are,

Frauds occur due to Unknown web address

Frauds occur due to unregistered MCC

Frauds occur due transactions above 100$

Frauds occur due to unregistered ISO response code

Research Problem and objectives

Credit card fraud occurs when a fraudster makes undesirable activities on consumers’ card information to make illegal profit. Credit card fraud affects many stakeholders including consumers as well as merchants and financial institutions. According to the Nilson Report in October 2016, more than $31 trillion were generated worldwide by online payment systems in 2015, increasing 7.3% than 2014. Worldwide losses from credit card fraud rose to $21 billion in 2015, and will possibly reach $31 billion by 2020. Our projects objectives include handing the imbalance of data, identifying fraud categories and their patterns, selecting the most suitable machine learning algorithms that best fit data, train a machine learning model with the highest accuracy and efficiency and implementing a fraud detection system

Literature survey and Research gap

Mainly Fraud analytics techniques are classified as Descriptive (Unsupervised) and Predictive (Supervised). Selection of the model is based on the performance of each algorithm which contains in each technique in the process of fraud detection and the nature of the dataset used as the sample dataset. Credit card data have the issue of skewed distribution which is also known as class imbalance. According to Andrea et al. their project addresses class imbalance including other issues such as concept drift and verification latency. They have also illustrated the most appropriate performance measures that can be used in fraud-detection. The achievement of the research also includes a formal model and an effective learning strategy for addressing the verification latency and an alert– feedback interaction. According to experiments they have claimed that, in contrast to traditional performance measures used in the literature the precision of the reported alerts is probably the most meaningful measure. In this research we follow supervised learning techniques and data analysis methodologies to come up with the most prominent machine learning models.

Methodology

This project focuses on four main types of credit card fraud. Selecting machine learning models which can capture each fraud is challenging. Furthermore the accuracy and performance of each model has to be taken into consideration. To overcome this we have followed the method as mentioned in figure 1. Number of techniques were used in the data analysis and optimal algorithms were selected by filtering them out comparatively against an appropriate performance matrix. Real-time credit card fraud detection is one of the main contributions of this project. Incoming transactions are interacted with the fraud detection model with the help of an API. A data warehouse is managed for data storing purposes and re training purposes.
